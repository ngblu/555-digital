# Objections Playbook — 555 Digital
> Living document. Update after every real call.
> Last updated: 2026-07-07

---

## 1. "My nephew / cousin / friend's kid does my website."
**Why they say it:** They don't want to hurt a family member's feelings or admit they made a bad call. There's also a sunk-cost feeling — someone already "handled" it.
**Response:** "That's great you've got someone helping. What I usually see is the nephew setup gets you online, but after that it sits — no updates, no SEO tweaks, no speed work. I can audit what's there for free and tell you honestly if it's holding you back. If it's solid, I'll tell you that too."

---

## 2. "I get all my work from word of mouth, I don't need a website."
**Why they say it:** Their business is working right now, so they see no urgency. They think a website is a brochure, not a sales tool.
**Response:** "Word of mouth is the best compliment you can get. Here's the thing — when someone hears about you, the first thing they do is Google you. If your site loads slow or looks outdated, that recommendation dies before they ever call. The website doesn't replace word of mouth, it catches the people your word of mouth sends your way."

---

## 3. "Can't I just use AI to build one for free?"
**Why they say it:** They've seen TikTok videos about AI website builders and think it's the same quality for zero cost.
**Response:** "AI can spit out a template in 30 seconds, absolutely. What it can't do is write city pages that actually rank in Crossville, or know that plumbers need a red emergency bar with a phone number, not a contact form. Those are the things that turn a visitor into a call. I've tested the AI builders — they get you 60% there, and the last 40% is what makes your phone ring."

---

## 4. "That's too expensive. I can get a Wix site for $15 a month."
**Why they say it:** They're comparing a custom build to a DIY subscription. Different product, same word — "website." They don't know what they're missing.
**Response:** "A Wix site is like a leased building with the landlord's sign still on it. You don't own it, you can't move it, and if Wix changes their pricing you're stuck. What I build you own outright. Plus, Wix doesn't do the SEO work, the speed optimization, or the local city pages. That's the stuff that actually brings in calls."

---

## 5. "My Facebook page is all I need."
**Why they say it:** They post there, get some engagement, and think that equals a web presence. They don't realize Facebook doesn't show up in Google searches like "plumber near me."
**Response:** "Facebook is great for showing your latest jobs to people who already know you. But when someone in Cookeville wakes up to a burst pipe at 6am, they're not scrolling Facebook — they're Googling 'emergency plumber Cookeville.' If you don't have a site that ranks for that search, you don't exist to that person."

---

## 6. "I'm too busy to deal with a website right now."
**Why they say it:** They're picturing months of meetings, content requests, and decisions they don't have time for. Past bad experiences with web people who needed constant hand-holding.
**Response:** "I get it — you're running a business. That's actually why I work the way I do. You give me your logo, your phone number, a few photos of your work, and I handle everything else. Most projects are done in 2-3 weeks, and I need maybe two calls from you total. You stay busy, I stay busy, and at the end you've got a site that works."

---

## 7. "I already have a site, it's fine."
**Why they say it:** The site loads on their phone and they can read it. They don't see the 5-second load time or the broken mobile layout because they know what to look for.
**Response:** "Let me show you something real quick — I ran your site through the same speed test Google uses. [show PageSpeed score]. Google penalizes slow sites in search results, and half your visitors leave before the page finishes loading. You're paying for that site every day in lost calls whether you realize it or not."

---

## 8. "I tried SEO once, it didn't work."
**Why they say it:** They got burned by a monthly-SEO plan that did nothing. They're skeptical of anything that smells like SEO.
**Response:** "Probably because most 'SEO services' are just a guy in another country dropping your business name into blog comments. What I do is different — I build individual pages for each city you serve, with real content about the work you do there. It's not magic, it's just doing the thing Google actually rewards. And you can see the results in your traffic numbers."

---

## 9. "I'll think about it" (repeatedly).
**Why they say it:** They're not a "no," but they're not ready to commit. Usually because they don't see the cost of inaction. Every week they wait is another week of lost calls.
**Response:** "Totally fair. Let me ask you this — if your site is costing you even two calls a week because people bounce before it loads, what does that add up to in a month? A plumbing call in this area is what, $300-500 minimum? Even one lost call a week pays for the whole website in a couple months. The free audit takes 10 minutes and you'll at least know what you're working with."

---

## 10. "I don't even know what to put on a website."
**Why they say it:** They think they need to write all the content, pick all the images, and design the whole thing. Overwhelmed before they start.
**Response:** "You don't need to know any of that. You tell me about your business, send me a few photos of your best work, and I handle everything — the writing, the layout, the SEO stuff. I've built these for plumbers, landscapers, detailers — I know what pages a service business needs. You approve it, I launch it."
